# HS8.302 / CTR-II

This repository contains reading notes and essay material for a course assignment on Robert M. Pirsig's *Zen and the Art of Motorcycle Maintenance*.

## Course contents in this folder

- `Zen and the Art of Motorcycle Maintenance...pdf` and `.txt`: the primary text used for reading and reference.
- `notes.md`: verification notes for key claims, concepts, and passages from the book.
- `zen_essay.md`: a more developed essay version focused on Pirsig's view of technology.

## Main themes

The work in this folder focuses on:

- the classical-romantic split in ways of understanding the world;
- technology as more than a neutral tool or mechanical object;
- the loss and recovery of craft, care, and attention;
- Pirsig's idea of Quality as something that resists strict definition;
- the limits of institutional rationality, described through the "Church of Reason";
- the connection between art, technology, and skilled practice.

## Reading focus

The central question running through the notes and essays is how technology can be reconnected with human values. Pirsig's answer is developed through motorcycle maintenance, writing, teaching, craft, and personal attention: good work depends not only on technical knowledge, but on care and presence.
